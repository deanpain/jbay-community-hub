## The One-Sentence Summary

A mobile app where J-Bay residents can find education, recreation, and entertainment opportunities — **without giving up their personal data**.

## What It Does

| Capability | Plain-language |
|------------|----------------|
| **Discover** | Browse what's available — tutoring, surf mentorship, youth events, skills programmes |
| **Verify** | Prove you're a resident or meet requirements without showing ID or address |
| **Participate** | Join programmes, attend events, track progress |
| **Govern** | Verified residents collectively decide how shared resources are used |

## Why Privacy Matters (and How It Works)

**The problem:** Today, signing up for community programmes means handing over personal data — ID numbers, proof of address, phone numbers. That data gets stored, shared, and can leak.

**The solution:** Zero-knowledge proofs (ZK) let you prove something without revealing the underlying data.

**Concrete example:** A young person can prove they live in J-Bay (using a verified credential from their school, church, or municipal record) without sharing their exact address, ID number, or phone number. The app sees only: "Verified resident: yes."

## FAQ

**Q: Is this replacing existing programmes?**  
A: No — it makes them easier to discover and join.

**Q: Does it cost money?**  
A: Free for residents. Funded through Catalyst (Cardano ecosystem grants).

**Q: What about data privacy?**  
A: Your data never leaves your phone. The app only sees proofs, not personal information.

**Q: Who controls it?**  
A: Verified residents. The project is structured as a community-governed platform, not owned by any single organisation.
